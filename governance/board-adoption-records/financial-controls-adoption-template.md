# Board Adoption Record — Template

> Status: Template — fill in and retain when the Board formally adopts a policy
> Last updated: 2026-06-29
> File: governance/board-adoption-records/financial-controls-adoption-template.md
>
> **This is a reusable adoption-record template.** A completed copy of this record is what makes a draft
> policy operative. Until a completed, signed (or minute-referenced) adoption record exists, the underlying
> policy remains a draft and is **not** legally binding on the Foundation.

---

## Purpose

Several Foundation policies are drafted but not yet operative — most notably
`governance/financial-controls-policy.md`, which carries the standing note *"not operative until formally
adopted by the Board of Directors."* This template provides the formal record that closes that gap.

Use one copy of this record per policy adoption. Store the completed record here in
`governance/board-adoption-records/` (public-safe — no signatures, home addresses, or personal data) and
reference it in the corresponding private Board minutes.

> **Private vs public:** The public adoption record states *that* the Board adopted a policy, *when*, and the
> *vote result*. Signed minutes containing personal signatures and home addresses remain private per
> `repo-structure.md` and `governance/financial-controls-policy.md` Section 13.

## Adoption Workflow

1. Place the policy draft on the Board meeting agenda (see `governance/compliance-calendar.md` for the
   annual review cadence).
2. Confirm quorum per the bylaws before any vote.
3. Confirm or revise all `[TBD]` placeholders (table above) before the motion to adopt.
4. Move, second, and vote to adopt. Record the result in the table above.
5. Secretary records the adoption in private signed minutes and completes this public-safe record.
6. Update the adopted policy's status header and Adoption Status table with the effective date.
7. Commit the completed adoption record and the updated policy to this repository.
