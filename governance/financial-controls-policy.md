## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes financial controls for Luminis Foundation to ensure responsible, transparent, and accountable use of Foundation funds. It applies to all financial transactions, including grants received, expenses paid, reimbursements, and any future revenue.

## 3. Board Oversight

The Board of Directors exercises ultimate fiduciary oversight of all Foundation finances.

- Financial activity summaries shall be presented at every scheduled Board meeting.
- Any expenditure exceeding [TBD — placeholder: $500] requires prior Board notification.
- Any expenditure exceeding [TBD — placeholder: $2,000] requires explicit prior Board approval.
- These thresholds are placeholders and shall be confirmed or revised by the Board at policy adoption.
- The Treasurer shall notify the President promptly of any transaction that appears unusual, unexpected, or inconsistent with the Foundation's stated activities.

---

## 4. Funds Handled by President and Treasurer

Funds are handled by the President and Treasurer under board oversight. The Foundation maintains a board-authorized institutional bank account. Account details are retained privately by authorized officers and are not committed to any public record.

- Only the President and Treasurer are authorized to initiate or approve payments on behalf of the Foundation.
- No payment method details (account numbers, card numbers, routing numbers, or online banking credentials) shall be committed to any public repository, shared document, or unencrypted file.

## 6. Debit Card Usage Policy

The Foundation may use a debit card associated with the institutional bank account for authorized Foundation expenditures.

- The debit card is issued to an authorized officer as designated by the President and Treasurer.
- The card is used only for pre-authorized Foundation expenditures consistent with the spending approval tiers in Section 5.
- Card account details, card numbers, and PINs are never committed to any repository, email, or unencrypted storage.
- Lost or suspected-compromised cards shall be reported to the issuing institution immediately and to the Board within 48 hours.

---

## 7. Receipt Retention

A receipt or invoice is required for every expenditure without exception.

- **Physical receipts** shall be retained in a physical file held by the Treasurer.
- **Digital copies:** All receipts shall be scanned as PDFs promptly after the transaction.
- **Digital receipt storage:** Scanned PDF copies are stored in private officer-controlled records accessible to the Treasurer and President. These records are not committed to any public repository.
- Receipts shall be retained for a minimum of [TBD — proposed: 7 years] consistent with IRS nonprofit record retention guidelines.

---

## 8. Monthly Financial Review

Monthly financial review is triggered when the Foundation account has more than **3 posted transactions in a calendar month**.

Transactions that count toward this threshold include:
- Deposits
- Withdrawals
- Debit card purchases
- Reimbursements
- Bank fees
- Transfers
- Grant deposits
- Donor receipts
- Vendor payments

Reviews may also be held earlier at the discretion of the President or Treasurer if activity is considered material, unusual, grant-related, or otherwise requires board attention.

**Review process:**
1. Treasurer prepares a summary of all transactions for the period.
2. President reviews and confirms accuracy.
3. Summary is retained in private officer-controlled records.
4. Any anomalies are reported to the Board at or before the next scheduled Board meeting.

---

## 9. Reimbursement Process

For any expenditure paid personally by an officer or volunteer and to be reimbursed by the Foundation:

1. Submit a written reimbursement request to the Treasurer (email is acceptable).
2. Attach receipt (photo or PDF).
3. Treasurer confirms the expenditure was pre-authorized and within policy.
4. President co-approves reimbursement.
5. Reimbursement is paid within [TBD — proposed: 30 days] of approval.
6. Receipt and reimbursement record are retained per Section 7.

---

## 10. Grant Fund Tracking

- Each grant received shall be assigned a tracking record in private officer-controlled files.
- Tracking record includes: funder name, grant amount, date received, purpose restrictions, reporting requirements, and running expenditure log.
- Grant funds must be used only for the purpose specified in the grant agreement.
- Restricted grant funds that cannot be used for their intended purpose must be returned to the funder or re-purposed with written funder approval.
- The President is responsible for grant reporting to funders; the Treasurer is responsible for grant fund tracking and reconciliation.

---

## 11. Prohibited Uses of Foundation Funds

Foundation funds shall not be used for:

- Personal expenses unrelated to Foundation activities
- Political contributions or lobbying activities prohibited under IRC §501(c)(3)
- Gifts to individuals, except as part of a documented charitable program activity
- Any expense that primarily benefits an officer or director personally
- Any expenditure inconsistent with the Foundation's stated charitable purposes

---

## 12. Conflict-of-Interest Handling

Any officer or director with a financial interest in a proposed expenditure must:

1. Disclose the interest to the Board in writing before the expenditure is approved.
2. Recuse from the approval decision.
3. The disclosure and recusal must be recorded in Board minutes.

This provision supplements the Foundation's separately adopted Conflict-of-Interest Policy.

---

## 13. Records Never Stored Publicly

The following shall never be committed to any public repository, shared publicly, or included in any public document:

- Bank account number
- Bank routing number
- Debit or credit card numbers
- Online banking login credentials or passwords
- EIN / Federal Employer Identification Number
- Pay.gov receipts or IRS payment confirmations
- Bank statements showing account balances or transaction details
- Receipt PDFs or scanned financial documents
- Individual officer home addresses beyond the already-public registered office

These records are maintained privately by authorized officers and disclosed to the Board upon request.

---

## 14. Annual Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed by the Board of Directors at least annually, at the first Board meeting of each calendar year. Revisions require Board approval. The effective date of each revision shall be recorded in Board minutes.
